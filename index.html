Short Bio

Lionel Messi, born on June 24, 1987, in Rosario, Argentina, is widely regarded as one of the greatest football players of all time. Messi began his football journey at a young age, starting off with Argentine club Newells Old Boys at 6 years old. Him and his father were approached by Carles Rexach, an FC Barcelona scout. The first contract/agreement was written on a napkin in a restaurant. Messi joined the youth ranks of FC Barcelona's renowned La Masia academy as a teenager. He made his first-team debut for Barcelona in 2004 at the age of 17, quickly establishing himself as a prodigious talent. Over the years, Messi has achieved numerous records and milestones. His extraordinary dribbling skills, vision, and goal-scoring prowess have earned him accolades and admiration from fans and peers alike.

Messi's partnership with Barcelona reached unprecedented heights, contributing significantly to the club's success. He played a key role in Barcelona's dominance in both domestic and international competitions, including winning multiple UEFA Champions League, domestic cups, and La Liga titles. Individually, Messi has been awarded the prestigious FIFA Ballon d'Or 8 times, solidifying his status as the best player in the world. In August 2021, Messi left Barcelona due to financial constraints faced by the club. Shortly afterward, he signed with Paris Saint-Germain (PSG), marking a new chapter in his illustrious career. After 2 years in Paris, Messi decided to join Inter Miami, a budding club in the MLS.
Messi has also been an integral part of the Argentina national team. Despite facing criticism early in his career for not winning major international trophies with Argentina, he secured a historic victory in the 2021 Copa America, providing him with a well-deserved triumph on the international stage. Soon after, he won the Finalissima with Argentina against Italy, winners of the 2020 Euros. On December 18th, 2022, he made history, winning the prestigious World Cup.

Beyond his football achievements, Messi is known for his humility and philanthropic work. He has been involved in various charitable activities, including the Leo Messi Foundation, which focuses on education and healthcare initiatives for vulnerable children.Lionel Messi's impact on football extends beyond the pitch, making him an iconic figure in the sport's history.

World Cup 2022

Messi played a crucial role in Argentina winning the most sought-after trophy in football.

Messi donned the captains armband, as he mostly does, for the Argentine side through the tournament.

Messi became the only player to ever win the Golden Ball in multiple World Cups.

Messi became the only player in history to win Man of the Match in every knockout stage.

Messi became the only player in history to score in each round of the knockout stages (RO16, quarterfinals, semifinals, and finals).
